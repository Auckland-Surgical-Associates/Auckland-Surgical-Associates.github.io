Acid reflux, also known as gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), occurs when stomach acid flows back into the esophagus.

**Common symptoms include:**
- Heartburn
- Regurgitation
- Chest pain
- Difficulty swallowing
- Chronic cough

**Contributing factors:**
- Lifestyle habits (diet, obesity, smoking)
- Certain medications
- Anatomical abnormalities like hiatal hernia

**Diagnosis typically involves:**
A combination of medical history, symptom assessment, physical examination, and diagnostic tests like endoscopy, pH monitoring, and barium X-rays.

**Management strategies:**
- Lifestyle modifications (avoiding trigger foods, eating smaller meals, maintaining healthy weight)
- Over-the-counter medications (antacids, H2 blockers)
- Prescription medications (proton pump inhibitors)
- Surgical interventions like fundoplication for severe cases

**Potential complications of untreated acid reflux:**
Untreated or poorly managed acid reflux can lead to complications such as esophagitis, Barrett's esophagus, and increased esophageal cancer risk.

**Follow-up care:** Regular monitoring with healthcare providers is essential to prevent complications.
