**Symptoms of Oesophageal Motility Disorders:**

- Dysphagia (difficulty swallowing)
- Chest pain not related to heart issues
- Regurgitation of food and liquids
- Sensation of food sticking in the throat
- Heartburn

**Treatment Options:**

**Lifestyle Changes:**
Avoiding trigger foods, eating smaller meals, and sleeping with the head elevated.

**Medication:**
Proton pump inhibitors (reduce stomach acid) and calcium channel blockers (relax oesophageal muscles).

**Therapeutic Procedures:**

- **Oesophageal dilation:** Widening the oesophagus using a balloon or dilators to relieve swallowing difficulties.

- **Botulinum toxin injections:** To relax overly tight oesophageal muscles.

**Surgery:**

Laparoscopic (Hellers Procedure) or endoscopic myotomy (POEM procedure) (cutting the muscles of the oesophagus) are considered for some cases.

Consulting a surgeon or gastroenterologist for an accurate diagnosis and tailored treatment plan is advised.
