"Wernicke encephalopathy is a severe neurological disorder caused by thiamine (Vitamin B1) deficiency, commonly associated with chronic alcoholism."

## Key Symptoms

- Confusion
- Ataxia (loss of coordination)
- Ocular abnormalities such as nystagmus (involuntary eye movements)

## Treatment

**Thiamine replacement therapy:** High-dose intravenous thiamine to replenish thiamine levels

**Proper nutrition:** A balanced diet rich in thiamine for recovery and prevention

**Monitoring and management of complications:** Patients may require monitoring for other nutritional deficiencies and complications such as seizures or cardiac abnormalities

"Early recognition and intervention are vital for improving outcomes in Wernicke encephalopathy."
