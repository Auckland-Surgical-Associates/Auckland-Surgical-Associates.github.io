**What is it?**
A hiatus hernia occurs when a portion of the stomach protrudes into the chest cavity through the diaphragm, leading to various symptoms.

**Symptoms include:**
- Heartburn
- Chest pain
- Difficulty swallowing
- Regurgitation of food or liquid
- Belching
- Sensation of fullness after eating

**Treatment approaches:**

*Lifestyle modifications:* Avoiding large meals, acidic or spicy foods, and alcohol. Elevating the head of the bed and maintaining a healthy weight may reduce symptoms.

*Medications:* Antacids, proton pump inhibitors (PPIs), and H2-receptor antagonists can decrease stomach acid production and alleviate heartburn.

*Surgical intervention:* In severe cases when lifestyle changes and medications prove ineffective, surgery may be necessary to repair the hernia and prevent complications.

**Key recommendation:** Consult a healthcare professional for accurate diagnosis and personalized treatment. Regular monitoring and follow-up are crucial for effective symptom management and complication prevention.
