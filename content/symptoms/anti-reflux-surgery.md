Anti-reflux surgery, or fundoplication, wraps the stomach around the esophagus to prevent acid reflux. Any hiatus hernia present needs to be repaired.

Candidates may have severe GERD symptoms despite medication or wish to avoid long-term medication.

Diagnostic tests confirm GERD and assess severity before surgery.

Risks include swallowing difficulty, bloating, and surgical complications.

Recovery involves a short hospital stay and gradual return to normal activities.

Success rates are high, with many patients experiencing symptom relief and reduced need for medication.
