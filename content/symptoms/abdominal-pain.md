## Serious Abdominal Pain

**Characteristics:**
- Sudden and persistent severe pain
- Unrelieved by rest or over-the-counter medication

**Alarming accompanying symptoms:**
- Fever
- Vomiting blood
- Difficulty breathing
- Fainting

**Possible conditions:**
- Appendicitis: Sharp pain migrating to lower right abdomen, nausea, vomiting, fever
- Bowel obstruction: Severe cramping, bloating, constipation
- Gallstones or pancreatitis: Abdominal pain with jaundice
- Pancreatic conditions: Severe, stabbing pain in upper abdomen radiating to back

## Non-Serious Abdominal Pain

**Characteristics:**
- Mild and short-lived discomfort
- Self-resolving without medical intervention

**Common causes:**
- Gas
- Indigestion
- Muscle strain
- Overeating or spicy foods
- Menstrual cramps

**Relief methods:**
- Rest
- Over-the-counter pain relievers
- Dietary modifications
