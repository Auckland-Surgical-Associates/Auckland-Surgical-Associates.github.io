**Cardiopulmonary exercise testing (CPET)** is an advanced tool used to evaluate the efficiency and interaction of the heart, lungs, and muscles during exercise.

## Purpose:

CPET assesses how well the body uses oxygen and eliminates carbon dioxide, providing a comprehensive evaluation of cardiovascular and pulmonary responses to physical stress.

## Procedure:

The test usually involves exercising on a treadmill or stationary bike while connected to equipment that monitors breathing, oxygen usage, carbon dioxide output, and heart activity.

## Key Measurements:

- Oxygen consumption (VO2 max)
- Carbon dioxide production
- Heart rate and rhythm
- Breathing patterns and lung function

## Applications:

- Diagnosing unexplained exercise intolerance
- Investigating symptoms such as shortness of breath and fatigue
- Assessing the severity and impact of cardiovascular and pulmonary conditions
- Evaluating fitness levels for major surgery and tailor exercise and rehabilitation programs

## Benefits:

- Provides detailed, objective data that helps in accurate diagnosis and management
- Helps in formulating personalized treatment and conditioning plans
- Useful in both clinical settings and athletic performance optimization

CPET is invaluable for healthcare professionals to better understand and manage a variety of health conditions.
